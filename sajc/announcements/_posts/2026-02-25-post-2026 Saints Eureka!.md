<h2><strong>The Spirit of Inquiry at Saints Eureka! 2026</strong></h2>
<p>The field was abuzz with energy this week as our JC1 students took part
in a long-standing college tradition: <strong>Saints Eureka!</strong> This
science-based challenge serves as a significant milestone for our newest
Saints, moving them from the excitement of Orientation into a space where
curiosity and collaboration take center stage.</p>
<p></p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">

<h3><strong>The Challenge: Engineering with Purpose</strong> </h3>
<p>This year, classes were tasked with constructing air-propelled rockets
using only recyclable materials. The competition pushed students to think
holistically—balancing the technical aerodynamics required for the "Longest
Flight" with the creative flair needed for the "Best Design" category.</p>
<p></p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">

<p></p>
<h3><strong>Building More Than Rockets</strong> </h3>
<p>Beyond the mechanics of flight, the day was truly about the chemistry
of a new class coming together. Working in teams, students had to navigate
different ideas and perspectives, practicing the humility and respect that
turn a group of individuals into a unified community. The execution of
the event was also a testament to the college's culture of mentorship,
with JC2 seniors stepping up to guide and support their juniors throughout
the challenge.</p>
