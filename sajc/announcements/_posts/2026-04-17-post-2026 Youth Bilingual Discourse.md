<p>For the third consecutive year, SAJC proudly organised the Youth Bilingual
Discourse in collaboration with the National Library Board.</p>
<p></p>
<p>Held on 16 April 2026 (Thursday), this year’s event brought together about
150 participants from 13 junior colleges and centralised institutions.
In preparation, 40 students from across the participating schools stepped
up as student facilitators and underwent a facilitation training on 25
March 2026 (Wednesday), where they were equipped with the polarity management
framework and developed the confidence to lead meaningful discussions on
current issues in both their Mother Tongue Languages and English.</p>

<p>
<br>The discourse commenced with a facilitation briefing by our Level Head
of Chinese Language, Mr Gabriel Tien, who set the tone for thoughtful and
respectful dialogue. Participants were then inspired by a keynote address
from Mediacorp News Presenter, Ms Tan Qin Lin, who highlighted the value
of bilingualism in both professional settings and everyday interactions
with family.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 85%;" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Announcements/2026 Youth Bilingual Discourse/WhatsApp_Image_2026_04_20_at_17_04_41__2_.jpg">
</div>
<p></p>
<p>Throughout the session, student-facilitators guided participants to reflect
on how critical thinking and self-expression can differ across languages,
deepening their appreciation of bilingual communication.</p>
<p>The programme concluded with a lively takeaway segment led by our Lead
Teacher of Malay Language, Dr Pitchay Gani, where student-facilitators
took turns to share their key insights.</p>
<p>
<br>From the reflections gathered, it is clear that participants not only
gained a deeper understanding of the polarity thinking framework, but also
cherished the opportunity to connect with like-minded youths from across
JCs through the shared experience of bilingualism.</p>
