<h1>Commemorating Total Defence Day 2026 with Commitment to Resilience and Remembrance</h1>
<p>The college marked Total Defence Day 2026 with a series of experiential
learning activities designed to strengthen students’ adaptability, crisis
readiness and sense of collective responsibility.</p>

<p>Within the College, students navigated simulated disruptions, including
a digital blackout scenario that encouraged greater face-to-face collaboration
and calm problem-solving. Through the interactive “Project BLACKOUT!” role-plays,
students assumed the roles of crisis responders, applying critical thinking
and teamwork in realistic situations. These activities reinforced the importance
of remaining composed and united in times of uncertainty.</p>
<p>Beyond the College, a group of students represented SAJC at the <strong>59th War Memorial Service in Commemoration of the Civilian Victims of the Japanese Occupation and Total Defence</strong>,
held at War Memorial Park. Organised by the Singapore Chinese Chamber of
Commerce and Industry, the service was graced by Guest of Honour Desmond
Lee, Minister for Education and Minister-in-Charge of Social Services Integration.</p>

<p>The solemn ceremony provided students with the opportunity to pay tribute
to the civilian victims of the Japanese Occupation and to reflect on the
enduring significance of Total Defence in safeguarding Singapore’s future.</p>
<p>Through both commemoration and active participation, the day underscored
an important message. Resilience is built through preparation. Unity strengthens
communities. Empathy guides our response in moments of challenge. These
values remain central to the character and spirit of the SAJC community.</p>
<p>Together, we stay ready. Together, we stay strong.</p>
